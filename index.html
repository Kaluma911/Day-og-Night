Blockout

The blockout/mockup is like the first draft of our project. We use basic shapes and outlines to plan where things will go in our scene, like buildings, objects, and people. It helps us see if everything fits together the way we want before we add all the details. This stage is important because it lets us try out different ideas quickly and see what works best. We can move things around easily and make changes without getting stuck on small stuff. Even though the blockout/mockup looks simple, it's the starting point for making our scene look awesome. It helps us figure out the big picture and make sure everything makes sense before we add all the finishing touches.

Clay Render

The clay render is like a model made of plain clay. It's a simple version of our scene without any colors or fancy details. We use it to focus on the shapes and forms of objects and characters. In the clay render, everything is the same color, like it's made of clay. This helps us see the overall shape and structure more clearly. We can check if everything looks right and if any adjustments are needed before we add all the colors and textures. Even though it's not as pretty as the final version, the clay render is really helpful. It lets us make sure everything looks good from all angles and that nothing is missing or out of place.

Wireframe

Imagine our project as a skeleton. The wireframe is like the bones that support everything. It's a simple outline made of lines and shapes that show us where things are in our scene. We use it to plan the structure and layout before adding all the details. With the wireframe, we can see the basic shape of buildings, objects, and characters. It helps us understand how they fit together and move around. Think of it as a blueprint that guides us in building our scene step by step. Even though it looks basic, the wireframe is super important. It helps us make sure everything is in the right place and works well together before we add all the fancy colors and textures.
